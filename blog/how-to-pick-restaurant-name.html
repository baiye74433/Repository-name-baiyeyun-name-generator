How to Pick a Restaurant Name That Works

Your restaurant name appears on menus, delivery apps, signage, and Google. Here is how to choose one that brings customers through the door.

Why Restaurant Names Matter More Than You Think

When someone searches "restaurants near me," your name appears alongside dozens of competitors. A clear, appetizing name can be the difference between a click and a scroll-past. Your name also needs to work on DoorDash, Uber Eats, Google Maps, and Instagram.

How to Choose the Right Name

1. Match the Name to the Experience

A fine dining restaurant needs a name that sounds elegant. A taco truck needs something fun and approachable. The name should prepare customers for what they will find inside.

2. Make It Easy to Find

If someone hears your restaurant name from a friend, they should be able to type it into Google and find you immediately. Avoid unusual spellings and words that are hard to search for.

3. Check Delivery App Availability

In 2026, a huge percentage of restaurant orders come through delivery apps. Make sure your name works on DoorDash, Uber Eats, and Grubhub. Short, clear names perform better on these platforms.

4. Consider Your Location

Including your neighborhood or city in the name can help with local SEO. "The Elm Street Kitchen" automatically ranks for "restaurant on Elm Street" searches.

Common Restaurant Naming Mistakes

  • Too generic — "The Restaurant" or "Good Food" tells customers nothing.
  • Hard to spell — If customers cannot spell it, they cannot find it on Google.
  • Too similar to a nearby restaurant — Check Google Maps before committing.
  • Limiting your menu — "Joe's Burgers" struggles if you want to add pizza later.

What to Do After Choosing a Name

  1. Register on Google Business immediately.
  2. Create an Instagram account. Food photography drives restaurant discovery.
  3. Register on DoorDash, Uber Eats, and Grubhub.
  4. Design a logo that works on signage, menus, and delivery app thumbnails.
