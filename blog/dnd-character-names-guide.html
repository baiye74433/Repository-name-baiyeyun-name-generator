DnD Character Names: A Complete Naming Guide

Your D&D character name is the first thing your party learns about you. Here is how to choose a name that fits your race, class, and backstory.

Why D&D Names Matter

In Dungeons & Dragons, your character name appears on every session, every character sheet, and every story your party tells afterward. A great name helps you roleplay, helps your DM remember you, and helps the party connect with your character.

Names by Race

Human

Human names are the most flexible. They can be medieval European, fantasy-inspired, or completely invented. Examples: Aldric, Mira, Theron, Elise.

Elf

Elf names flow with soft consonants and long vowels. They sound elegant and ancient. Examples: Aelindra, Thalion, Lirael, Galadros.

Dwarf

Dwarf names are short, strong, and often include hard consonants. They sound like stone and metal. Examples: Thorin, Dwalin, Gimli, Bofur.

Orc

Orc names are harsh and aggressive. They use guttural sounds and sharp consonants. Examples: Gruk, Azog, Bolg, Shagrat.

Halfling

Halfling names are warm, friendly, and often slightly humorous. Examples: Bilbo, Frodo, Samwise, Merry.

Tiefling

Tiefling names often have infernal influences. They can be dark and mysterious. Examples: Zariel, Moloch, Nyx, Kael.

Names by Class

  • Wizard: Names that sound scholarly and ancient: Aldric, Meridia, Tharion
  • Fighter: Names that sound strong and direct: Roderick, Valeria, Garrick
  • Rogue: Names that sound quick and shadowy: Nyx, Shade, Riven, Kael
  • Cleric: Names that sound holy and wise: Seraphiel, Celestine, Alden
  • Barbarian: Names that sound primal and fierce: Gruk, Thane, Kira, Ragnar
  • Bard: Names that sound charming and musical: Lyric, Cadence, Aria, Finn

Tips for D&D Naming

  1. Match the race's naming convention — An orc named "Flowerpetal" breaks immersion.
  2. Keep it pronounceable — Your DM and party will say this name hundreds of times.
  3. Add a title or epithet — "Aldric the Bold" is more memorable than just "Aldric."
  4. Consider the backstory — A noble-born character has a different name style than a street urchin.
