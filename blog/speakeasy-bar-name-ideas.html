Speakeasy Bar Name Ideas for Hidden Lounges and Cocktail Concepts

Naming a speakeasy or hidden cocktail bar? Use these tips to create a name that feels secretive, stylish, and memorable.

Lean into mystery without becoming vague

A speakeasy name should suggest discovery, exclusivity, or after-hours energy. It can hint at secrecy without becoming so abstract that people cannot remember it. The best names feel like an invitation.

Use the room, entrance, or neighborhood as inspiration

Hidden bars often have strong physical details: a side door, basement stair, velvet hallway, alley entrance, rooftop staircase, or private code. Those details can become the most memorable part of the name.

Keep it elegant enough for menus and reservations

Speakeasy concepts often rely on visual presentation. The name should look good on menus, booking pages, neon signage, and social media posts. Stylish simplicity usually works better than overdone noir language.

Test whether the name feels premium when spoken aloud

A name may look clever on paper but sound weak when a guest recommends it. Say it out loud in a sentence like, “Let’s meet at ___ tonight,” and see if it still sounds strong.
