How to Start a Podcast: Choosing the Perfect Name

Your podcast name is the first thing potential listeners see in search results and on social media. Here is how to get it right.

Why Podcast Names Matter for Discovery

When someone searches Apple Podcasts or Spotify for a topic, your show name appears alongside hundreds of others. A clear, descriptive name helps you rank for relevant searches. A clever but vague name gets buried.

Podcast Name Styles

Descriptive Names

Tell listeners exactly what they get: "The Marketing Show," "True Crime Daily," "Startup Stories." These rank well in search but can feel generic.

Clever Names

Wordplay, puns, or cultural references: "Serial," "Radiolab," "How I Built This." Memorable but harder to discover through search.

Personal Names

Use your name: "The Tim Ferriss Show," "The Joe Rogan Experience." Works if you already have an audience.

Brand Names

A standalone word or phrase: "Revolutions," "Lore," "Criminal." Highly brandable but requires marketing investment.

The Best Approach for New Podcasters

If you are starting from zero, combine a descriptive subtitle with a creative main title. For example: "Lore: Dark Stories from History" or "Startup: A Podcast About Building a Business." This gives you both discoverability and personality.

Common Podcast Naming Mistakes

  • Too vague — "The Show" tells nobody what it is about.
  • Too long — Long names get truncated in podcast apps.
  • Too similar to existing shows — Search Apple Podcasts before committing.
  • Hard to spell — If someone hears it in a recommendation, they need to be able to find it.
