How to Name Your Brand: A Step-by-Step Guide

Your brand name is the foundation of everything you build. Here is a clear, practical process for choosing a name that works today and scales tomorrow.

Brand Name vs. Business Name

A business name is what you register with the government. A brand name is what customers remember. Sometimes they are the same. Often they are not. Nike's business name is Nike, Inc., but the brand is just "Nike." Apple's business name is Apple Inc. The brand is "Apple."

When you are naming your brand, you are choosing the word that will appear on products, packaging, social media, and in your customers' conversations. It needs to be short, memorable, and emotionally resonant.

Types of Brand Names

1. Descriptive Names

Names that describe what the company does: General Motors, PayPal, The Home Depot. These are clear but can feel generic.

2. Invented Names

Made-up words that become brands: Spotify, Kodak, Xerox. These are highly brandable but require marketing investment to build recognition.

3. Evocative Names

Names that evoke a feeling: Amazon (vast), Patagonia (adventure), Uber (superior). These work well when the feeling matches the product.

4. Lexical Names

Wordplay, puns, or creative spellings: Tumblr, Lyft, Reddit. These are catchy but can be hard to spell.

5. Founder Names

Named after a person: Ford, Chanel, Disney. These carry personal legacy but can be limiting if the founder leaves.

The 5-Step Brand Naming Process

Step 1: Define Your Brand Position

Write a one-sentence brand position: "We are the [what] for [who] that [why it matters]." For example: "We are the coffee brand for busy professionals that delivers premium taste in under 60 seconds."

Step 2: Generate 50+ Candidates

Do not stop at 5 or 10. Push past the obvious ideas. The best brand names usually come after the 30th or 40th idea. Use a brand name generator to speed up this process.

Step 3: Apply the "Bar Test"

Imagine telling someone your brand name in a noisy bar. Can they hear it, understand it, and spell it? If not, it fails the bar test.

Step 4: Check Legal Availability

Search the trademark database, check domain availability, and verify social media handles. A name that is legally unavailable is not a name, it is a lawsuit waiting to happen.

Step 5: Test with Real People

Show your top 5 names to people who are not your friends or family. Ask them: "What do you think this company does?" Their answers will tell you if the name communicates the right message.

Brand Naming Mistakes to Avoid

  • Choosing a name that limits growth — "Boston Cupcakes" cannot easily expand to cookies or national shipping.
  • Picking a name too similar to competitors — You want to stand out, not create confusion.
  • Falling in love with a name before checking availability — Always check domains and trademarks first.
  • Making it too complicated — If you need to explain the name, it is too complicated.
  • Rushing the decision — Spend at least a week living with your top choices before committing.
